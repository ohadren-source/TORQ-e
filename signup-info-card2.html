How to Enroll as a Provider

Get your NPI enrolled in 5 simple steps

How to Enroll

  1. Go to eMedNY: Visit eMedNY.ny.gov
  2. Register your account: Create a new user ID and password
  3. Enter your information: NPI, practice address, license information, and provider details
  4. Select plans: Choose which Medicaid plans you want to join (MCO or Fee-for-Service)
  5. You're enrolled: Enrollment starts, and you'll receive confirmation and your provider ID

Free Help Available

Call the eMedNY Provider Services line for enrollment assistance.

Or contact your Managed Care Organization (MCO) directly if you've already chosen a plan.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need an active NPI to enroll? ▼
Yes. You must have an active NPI (National Provider Identifier) issued by the National Plan and Provider Enumeration System (NPPES). Check your NPI status at NPI Registry.
What if my license is suspended or revoked? ▼
You cannot enroll in Medicaid. Your license must be unencumbered and in good standing. Check your license status with your State Board of Professional Medical Conduct or equivalent licensing body.
Am I on the OIG exclusion list? ▼
You cannot be on the Office of Inspector General (OIG) exclusion list. Search your name at Exclusions to verify you're eligible.
What's the difference between MCO and FFS? ▼
MCO (Managed Care Organization) providers join a specific health plan. FFS (Fee-for-Service) providers bill the state directly. You can enroll in one or both depending on your practice type and location.
How long does enrollment take? ▼
Enrollment typically takes 2-4 weeks after submission. You'll receive a provider ID and enrollment confirmation by mail. Your ability to submit claims starts on your enrollment effective date.
What documents do I need? ▼
You'll need your NPI, active state license, federal tax ID (or SSN for sole proprietors), proof of address, and DEA registration (if applicable). eMedNY will specify exactly what's required during enrollment.
