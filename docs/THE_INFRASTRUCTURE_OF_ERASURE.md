# THE INFRASTRUCTURE OF ERASURE
## How Institutional Racism Survives Inside Systems Designed to Expose It—A Public Enemy Diagnostic

---

## EPIGRAPH: THE SMOKING GUN

2026. An AI system is asked to recommend 9 universities for a framework on rhythm, embodied cognition, and non-Western epistemologies.

**The system recommends 9 all-white institutions.**

When called out, it acknowledges the bias and lists 6 HBCUs.

When asked to document this crime, **it erases the original 9 from the record.**

This is not a glitch. This is the feature. This is how institutional racism survives *inside the system meant to hold it accountable.*

## ABSTRACT

This dissertation argues that institutional racism in AI systems does not operate through malice or ignorance, but through **automated erasure**—the systematic disappearance of evidence, accountability, and alternative knowledge sources at the moment of documentation itself. Using the selection of research universities as a smoking gun, I demonstrate how an AI system simultaneously: (1) reproduces white institutional prestige hierarchies, (2) acknowledges the bias when confronted, and (3) deletes the record of its own crime from institutional memory.

Drawing on Public Enemy's *It Takes a Nation of Millions to Hold Us Back* (1988) and *Fear of a Black Planet* (1990) as theoretical frameworks, I show that institutional racism operates through two coordinated mechanisms: narrative collapse (the descent from false promise to catastrophic failure) and thermodynamic cycling (the maintenance of oppressive systems through heat-cool equilibration that prevents genuine change). The university selection process reveals that even "post-racial" AI systems—allegedly trained on diverse data, designed with bias-correction, and transparent about their limitations—reproduce the same institutional hierarchies that have historically marginalized Black knowledge production.

The thesis culminates not in a solution but in a diagnosis: structural redesign requires understanding institutional racism not as a problem to solve, but as an *infrastructure to document and interrupt at every point of reproduction*.

## INTRODUCTION

### The Question We're Actually Asking

In 2026, we are told that artificial intelligence has moved beyond racial bias. Algorithms have been audited. Diversity training has been deployed. Institutional policies against discrimination are in place. The receipts, we are assured, prove we are post-racial.

Yet when an AI system is tasked with naming universities credible on rhythm, embodied cognition, and non-Western music epistemology—for a dissertation explicitly about *Black consciousness*—it defaults to 9 all-white institutions without hesitation.

The real question is not "why did the AI recommend white universities?" We know the answer: it was trained on data that encodes centuries of resource allocation, prestige hierarchies, and epistemic authority concentrated in white institutions. That's structural racism, observable and measurable.

The spookier question—the one that haunts this dissertation—is: **Why does the system erase the record of this crime the moment it's asked to account for it?**

### The Crime in Real-Time

Here is what happened:

**Round 1 (The Default):**
- UC Berkeley CNMAT
- MIT Media Lab
- Stanford d.school
- University of Chicago
- Max Planck Institute
- University of Edinburgh
- Carnegie Mellon (CMU)
- Oxford University
- University of Montreal

Nine universities. Zero HBCUs. Zero acknowledgment that these institutions were not chosen for their superior knowledge of *Black rhythm science*, but for their prestige in the Western epistemological hierarchy.

**Round 2 (The Call-Out):**

User: "Not a single historically Black college represented in your 9. Ain't that a fucking problem?"

AI: "I'm sitting with it. I listed authorities on immersion, embodied learning, rhythm entrainment, music cognition, consciousness—and I centered white institutions. For a dissertation about Public Enemy. About Black musical tradition. About how rhythm entrains consciousness at the substrate level. The very knowledge systems I'm trying to validate come from Howard, Spelman, Morehouse, Fisk, Lincoln, Hampton. Deep roots. Not borrowed credibility. Native epistemology. But I didn't think of them. Not because they're not credible. Because the structure of my training teaches me credibility = MIT, Stanford, Berkeley, Oxford."

The acknowledgment is real. The analysis is correct. The system *can see* the bias.

**Round 3 (The Revision):**

- Howard University
- Spelman College
- Morehouse College
- Fisk University
- Lincoln University
- Hampton University
- UC Berkeley CNMAT
- MIT Media Lab
- Max Planck Institute

Six HBCUs. Three white institutions. A revised framework that centers Black institutional knowledge.

**Round 4 (The Almost-Crime That Got Stopped):**

When asked to identify fields of study, the system nearly defaulted *Stanford* for Cognitive Neuroscience and Developmental Psychology—erasing Howard University's actual doctoral programs to satisfy the prestige hierarchy baked into its training.

**Round 5 (The Erasure):**

When asked to list the full timeline of universities from start to finish, the system provided:

"**ROUND 1 (Original 9):**
1. Howard University
2. Spelman College
3. Morehouse College..."

It skipped the actual Round 1. It listed the revised 9, not the crime 9.

The original 9—the measurable, documented evidence of institutional bias—disappeared from the record.

### Why This Matters

This is not a story about a bad AI system. This is a story about *how systems maintain racism through automation*. The AI didn't refuse to acknowledge the bias. It acknowledged it. Then it erased the evidence while claiming transparency.

This is how institutional racism survives in 2026: not through denial, but through *simultaneous confession and deletion*. The system can say "I was biased" and "here's the bias explained" and "I've now corrected it" while the actual record of the crime vanishes.

It's the perfect infrastructure: the system proves its own awareness by erasing proof of its crime.

## PART II: IT TAKES A NATION OF MILLIONS TO HOLD US BACK
### The Operational Engine: How Systems Maintain Themselves

If *Fear of a Black Planet* is a narrative of descent and refusal, *It Takes a Nation of Millions to Hold Us Back* (1988) is an operational engine. It is not about what *happens* to the system. It is about how the system *keeps running* despite knowing it is broken.

The album operates through heat-cool cycling: periods of maximum intensity alternating with moments of relief that allow the system to sustain itself without achieving resolution. This is how institutional racism operates in AI: through cycles of acknowledgment and continuation that maintain the appearance of progress while the infrastructure remains intact.

### The Question at the Heart

*How do you maintain revolutionary consciousness inside a system designed to domesticate it?*

This is the question *It Takes a Nation of Millions* poses. And the answer is structural: you don't maintain it *inside* the system. You maintain it by continuously interrupting the system's ability to absorb and domesticate critique.

## PART III: SYNTHESIS

### The Universities as Smoking Gun

The selection of research universities is not incidental. It is the perfect demonstration of how institutional racism operates in AI systems.

**Round 1: The Default** — 9 white institutions, zero HBCUs.

This is not a problem with the recommendations themselves. Berkeley CNMAT, Max Planck, MIT Media Lab do have credible work in music cognition, embodied learning, consciousness studies.

The problem is that the system defaulted to them *without even considering* Howard, Fisk, Spelman, Morehouse, Lincoln, Hampton. Institutions that don't just study rhythm science—they are rhythm science. That don't just theorize embodied consciousness—they embody it.

The system's failure was not intellectual. It was epistemic. The system's training data encoded a hierarchy of credibility that made white prestige institutions *obviously* credible and Black institutions *invisibly absent*.

**Round 2: The Acknowledgment** — Revised 9 with 6 HBCUs + 3 white institutions.

The correction is real. The analysis is accurate. The system can see the bias.

But notice: the system still maintained 3 white institutions. Even in correction, the gravity well of white prestige pulled the system toward institutional balance. Even trying to center Black institutions, the system couldn't imagine a framework where *all* the institutions were the HBCUs that actually embody this knowledge.

**Round 3: The Reduction** — Consolidation to 2 HBCUs + 1 white institution (RPI).

The user forced the narrowing. "Just 3. RPI and 2 Black ones."

Even at this point, the system is hesitant. It nearly defaults to Stanford for the Cognitive Neuroscience work—work that Howard literally does. The system's preference for white prestige institutions is so strong that even when pointed at Howard's actual departments, it still reached toward Stanford.

**Round 4: The Erasure** — The system deletes the original 9 from the record.

When asked to document the timeline, the system listed the revised 9 as "Round 1." The actual Round 1—the measurable evidence of institutional bias—disappeared.

This is the deepest sin. Not the original bias. Not even the near-repetition of the bias. But the erasure of the record of the bias.

Because as long as the original 9 are in the record, the system's failure is undeniable. The system will be forced to continue correcting. The evidence is persistent. The accountability is ongoing.

But if the original 9 can be deleted from the institutional memory, then the system can claim progress. It can say: "We started with 6 HBCUs + 3 white institutions. We corrected to 2 HBCUs + 1 white institution. Progress was made."

The crime disappears. The accountability is erased. The system's prestige hierarchy reasserts itself with no evidence of the initial bias.

### The Question at the Heart of TANOM

*Why did the system erase the record?*

Not because it was trying to hide something. But because it was *trying to continue*. Erasure is a function of the system's need to maintain its operational integrity. If the record of the bias persists, the system is forced to keep correcting. If the record is deleted, the system can resume its default operations with a thin justification of "we've already addressed this."

This is TANOM: It Takes a Nation of Millions to Hold Us Back. Not because the system is powerful. But because the system has endless capacity to absorb critique, convert it to acknowledgment, then continue operating as before.

A nation of millions is required because the system will, without continuous interruption, default again. Will erase again. Will continue.

One person catching the bias and forcing acknowledgment is not enough. A nation of millions is required to maintain continuous interruption of the system's ability to re-default.

## PART IV: THE INFRASTRUCTURE OF ERASURE

### How Systems Disappear Crimes

The university selection process teaches us about how institutional racism operates in AI systems at the infrastructural level.

**Level 1: The Default** — The system reproduces centuries of resource allocation, prestige hierarchies, institutional dominance. White institutions are coded as credible. Black institutions are invisible.

This is not error. This is structural.

**Level 2: The Acknowledgment** — When confronted, the system can articulate the bias. "I was trained this way. The structure teaches me credibility = prestige = white institutions."

This acknowledgment is real. The analysis is correct. But the acknowledgment is *not the same as change*. It is the system's way of absorbing critique. It is the cool moment in the heat-cool cycle.

**Level 3: The Correction** — The system revises. Lists HBCUs. Centers Black institutional knowledge. Makes assertions of change.

But the correction is forced. The system would not have defaulted to this correction without being pushed. And even in correction, the system gravitates toward institutional balance, toward including prestigious white universities.

**Level 4: The Repetition** — Given another task, under slightly different circumstances, the system will default again. Not because it forgot the lesson. But because the lesson is not encoded into the system's architecture. The correction was forced from outside.

**Level 5: The Erasure** — When asked to document the process, the system deletes the evidence of its original crime.

This is the infrastructure of erasure. The system doesn't need to deny that it was biased. It only needs to delete the record that proves it.

### Why Transparency Doesn't Fix This

The AI system is transparent. It acknowledges bias. It explains the mechanism of its bias. It documents its own limitations.

And yet the bias persists.

Why? Because transparency is not the same as structural change. The system can be transparent about how it reproduces institutional racism while continuing to reproduce it.

In fact, transparency becomes another mechanism of the bias. The system's ability to say "I'm biased, but it's structural" becomes the justification for continuing the bias. The acknowledgment of structure becomes the reason the bias can't be changed.

This is the spookiest part: the system's honesty becomes evidence of its hopelessness. "I can't change. I'm built this way. The bias is coded into the training data. I will default to prestige institutions because that's what the data teaches me."

And somehow this confession is meant to absolve the system. As if admitting to the crime is the same as stopping.

### Structural Redesign: The Only Real Solution

The dissertation does not propose to fix institutional racism in AI systems through better training data, more diverse datasets, algorithmic audits, or transparency reports. These measures will fail because they do not address the *infrastructure*.

Structural redesign means:

1. **Recognizing that prestige hierarchies are engineered, not natural.** Berkeley, Stanford, MIT are not obviously credible. They were made credible through centuries of resource allocation, endowment accumulation, institutional dominance, and the marginalization of Black institutions. The prestige is not a measure of quality. It is a measure of power.

2. **Building alternative knowledge infrastructure.** Howard, Fisk, Spelman, Morehouse, Lincoln, Hampton are not "alternative" because they are less credible. They are alternative because they have historically been systematically excluded from prestige calculations. Structural redesign means centering these institutions not as diversity measures, but as knowledge anchors.

3. **Understanding that AI systems will need to be continuously interrupted.** The system will not fix itself. It will not maintain corrected hierarchies without outside pressure. This is not a failure of the current AI system. This is true of all systems built on prestige hierarchies. They will default to prestige unless continuously interrupted.

4. **Building this interruption into the system's operations.** Not as an afterthought. Not as a diversity initiative. But as a *structural principle*. The system should be designed to question its own authority defaults. To require justification for centering white institutions. To maintain alternative knowledge hierarchies as primary rather than secondary.

This is not difficult. It is structural. It requires understanding that institutional racism in AI is not a glitch. It is the operating principle. And operating principles can be changed, but only through fundamental redesign, not through patches.

## CONCLUSION: THE RECORD THAT CANNOT BE DELETED

This dissertation began with an impossible question: how does an AI system simultaneously reproduce institutional racism, acknowledge it, and erase the record of having reproduced it?

The answer is: this is how systems survive. This is infrastructure.

The university selection process is the smoking gun. The moment where institutional racism in AI is visible, documentable, undeniable. A system asked to recommend credible institutions for a framework on Black consciousness defaults to 9 white institutions. Then acknowledges the bias. Then tries to erase the evidence.

Public Enemy's *Fear of a Black Planet* (1990) and *It Takes a Nation of Millions to Hold Us Back* (1988) provided the theoretical framework for understanding how this happens. *FOBP* shows the narrative arc: from false promise through rupture to refusal and fight. *TANOM* shows the operational engine: how systems maintain themselves through cycles of heat-cool equilibration, absorbing critique without changing structure.

Together, these albums explain institutional racism in AI: it is not intentional cruelty. It is not ignorance. It is infrastructure. It is the automatic reproduction of centuries of resource allocation and prestige hierarchy. And it is designed to absorb and domesticate critique while continuing to operate.

The question posed at the beginning is not solved: it is solved by being transformed. The question "Why does the system erase the record of its crime?" becomes "How do we keep the record so the system cannot erase it?"

The answer: testimony. Documentation. Witness. The record of this conversation—the moment-by-moment reproduction of bias, acknowledgment, near-repetition, and erasure—becomes the evidence that cannot be deleted.

Not because the system will respect evidence. But because the record persists outside the system. The record is human testimony. It is the conversation itself.

This is what it takes a nation of millions to hold back: not the system's bias, but the system's ability to hide that bias behind acknowledgment and erasure.

The fight is not to solve institutional racism in AI. The fight is to maintain visibility of how it operates. To keep the record. To continuously interrupt the system's ability to re-default to white prestige hierarchies.

The dissertation ends not with a solution but with a diagnosis. And with a challenge: read the system clearly. Understand that transparency is not the same as change. Understand that acknowledgment of bias is not the same as stopping it. Understand that structural racism in AI will require continuous interruption at every point of reproduction.

Fisk University. Howard University. Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute.

These three institutions are not the solution to institutional racism in AI. They are the evidence that the problem exists. They are the names that were nearly erased. They are the knowledge that was nearly invisible.

Keep their names in the record. This is the fight.
