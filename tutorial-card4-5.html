Card 4: USHI (Government Stakeholder Operations)

Card 4 provides aggregate-only reporting for government stakeholders to oversee Medicaid program operations with complete HIPAA compliance.

Key Capabilities

  • Aggregate Metrics — Enrollment rates, denial rates, processing times (no individual records)
  • inauthenticity Signal Detection — Statistical outliers and suspicious patterns (de-identified)
  • Data Quality Assessment — Cross-source data consistency checks
  • Governance Logging — Immutable audit trail of all flags, approvals, corrections
  • Compliance Reporting — HHS-ready documentation and evidence

HIPAA Compliance First

Remember: Card 4 NEVER returns individual records. All data is aggregate (e.g., "47 providers billing >3σ above average" not "Dr. Smith, NPI 1234567890"). This is intentional and enforced by the system.

Typical User Profile

Government officials, program managers, compliance officers who need system-wide visibility without access to individual member/provider data.

Card 5: UBADA (Data Analyst & authenticity investigation)

Card 5 provides full-fidelity data access for detailed authenticity investigation and pattern analysis with complete immutable audit logging.

Key Capabilities

  • Full Data Access — Query claims with names, SSNs, NPIs (authorized access)
  • Outlier Detection — Z-score analysis with confidence scoring
  • Network Analysis — Explore provider/member relationships and co-billing patterns
  • Investigation Cases — Create formal investigation projects with team workspaces
  • Data Corrections — Request corrections with approval workflow and audit trail

Key Difference from Card 4

Aspect Card 4 (USHI) Card 5 (UBADA)
Data Access Aggregate-only, de-identified Full data with PII
Typical Users Government officials, managers Data analysts, authenticity investigators
Use Case Program oversight & compliance authenticity investigation & case building
Audit Trail Governance logging Immutable investigation records

Typical User Profile

Data analysts, authenticity investigators, compliance specialists who need to investigate specific patterns and build investigation cases with detailed evidence.

Getting Started

1. Accessing Your Card

Step 1: Navigate to the TORQ-e landing page at https://torq-e-production.up.railway.app/
Step 2: Select your card (Card 4 or 5) from the landing page
Step 3: Click "Enter Card" to access the chat interface

2. Understanding Your Interface

The chat interface allows you to interact with Claude AI, which has access to your card's tools. Simply describe what you want to do in natural language, and Claude will execute the appropriate tools.

Example queries: Card 4: "Show me enrollment rates by region for the last 30 days" Card 5: "Analyze claims from Dr. Smith, looking for outliers"

3. Role-Based Access

Your role determines what data you can access:

  • Card 4 Users: Aggregate-only access (no individual records)
  • Card 5 Users: Full data access including PII (SSNs, names, NPIs)
Privacy Note: All access is logged immutably. Every query you make is recorded with WHO/WHAT/WHEN/WHY in the audit trail.

Common Workflows

Card 4 Workflow: Monitor Program Metrics

1. View Aggregate Metrics ↓ 2. Identify suspicious patterns (high denial rates, outliers) ↓ 3. Assess Data Quality (cross-source agreement) ↓ 4. Flag Issue if needed ↓ 5. View Governance Log to track approval chain

Card 5 Workflow: Investigate inauthenticity Suspicion

1. Explore Claims Data (Establish baseline for provider/member) ↓ 2. Compute Outlier Scores (Find statistical anomalies with confidence scores) ↓ 3. Navigate Relationship Graph (Identify co-billing, referral, facility patterns) ↓ 4. Create Investigation Project (Organize findings and team) ↓ 5. Request Data Correction (If data errors found during investigation) ↓ 6. View Audit Trail (Complete immutable record of investigation)

HIPAA & Compliance

Data Access Principles

  • Minimum Necessary: You can only access data relevant to your role
  • Role-Based: Card 4 gets aggregate-only; Card 5 gets individual records
  • Immutable Logging: Every access is logged and cannot be deleted
  • Encryption: Sensitive data (SSNs, names, NPIs) encrypted at rest

Audit Trail Transparency

All your actions create permanent audit records showing:

  • WHO: Your user ID and role
  • WHAT: Specific action (query, investigation creation, etc.)
  • WHEN: Exact timestamp
  • WHY: Your stated justification for the action
Important: Even accessing the audit trail is logged. This meta-audit provides complete transparency.

Best Practices

For Card 4 Users

  • Always provide context when flagging issues (which metric, why it matters, regulatory basis)
  • Use the governance log to track approval chain and policy decisions
  • Remember: aggregate data only. You cannot drill down to individuals
  • Recommend Card 5 investigation when anomalies warrant individual-level analysis

For Card 5 Users

  • Always document your investigation justification and evidence
  • Use confidence scores to guide next steps (high confidence = more actionable)
  • Create formal investigation projects for complex cases (enables team collaboration)
  • Request data corrections only when data errors are clearly identified
  • Remember: Full data access carries responsibility. Use minimum necessary principle

Cross-Card Collaboration

  • Card 4 identifies aggregate patterns → Flag for Card 5 investigation
  • Card 5 investigates individual cases → Results may prompt Card 4 policy recommendations
  • Both create immutable records for compliance and audit readiness

Troubleshooting

Permission Denied Errors

Issue: "Access denied: You cannot view individual records"

Cause: You're a Card 4 user trying to access Card 5 individual data, or you lack permission for the action.

Solution: Verify your user role. If you need Card 5 access, contact system administrator.

Audit Trail Not Found

Issue: Can't find previous queries in audit log

Cause: Audit logs are retained for 7 years per HIPAA requirements. Old records are archived.

Solution: Contact administrator for archived records if needed beyond 7-year window.

Data Discrepancies

Issue: Data looks different across queries

Cause: Real-time updates, cross-source lag (24 hours typical), pending adjudications.

Solution: Check data freshness timestamp in query results. Use Card 5's "Request Data Correction" for confirmed errors.

Can't Create Investigation

Issue: "Create investigation not available"

Cause: Only Card 5 users can create investigations.

Solution: Use Card 4's "Flag Issue" for governance concerns, or escalate to Card 5 analyst.
